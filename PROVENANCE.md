# Provenance & Licensing

Muleta is a revival of Deloitte Consulting's discontinued **Bullfighter** (2003–2005).

## The words are free to use

Individual words and short phrases are **not copyrightable** (37 CFR § 202.1(a);
*Feist Publications v. Rural Telephone Service Co.*, 499 U.S. 340 (1991)), and a
jargon→plain substitution with few sensible options is independently unprotectable under
the merger doctrine. Muleta's jargon terms are therefore free for anyone to use.

What can carry *thin* copyright is a compilation's original **selection, coordination, and
arrangement**, plus any explanatory prose. Muleta's corpus is therefore **independently
compiled** — our own selection, our own tiered weights and notes — with sources cited for
**provenance, not permission**. We do not reproduce any single source's curated list
verbatim as our arrangement, nor its commentary.

## What we use vs. what we do not redistribute

- **Used:** the individual jargon terms and Bull weights, and the publicly documented
  scoring formula (a mathematical method, not an expressive work).
- **Not redistributed:** the proprietary Bullfighter installer (`bullfighter.exe`,
  © Business Idiots LLC for Deloitte Consulting) and its embedded Access `.mdb`. Those are
  kept only as local research artifacts and are excluded from this repository.

## Sources

- **Dictionary + scoring formula** — recovered from the shipped Bullfighter 1.2 installer
  and the vendor scorecard, via the Internet Archive Wayback Machine
  (`fightthebull.com`, captures 2005 and 2009). See `docs/recovery-notes.md`.
- **Corpus weights** — tier-assigned by us, anchored to the vendor's three confirmed
  values (`global`=1, `leverage`=high, `envisioneer`=10).

## Corpus term provenance

Every entry in `data/jargon.yaml` carries a `source` field:
`extracted` (from the recovered Bullfighter dictionary) · `reconstructed`
(from documentation/press) · `user` · `llm-suggested`.
